Our History

Where it all started

Beans Coffee Stop Limited was the brainchild of Phil & Karen Gilbert during late 2003, and meticulous planning lead to the opening of the first Beans in Oundle on the 16th of February 2004. This shop was an overnight success and was quickly joined by a second, in the centre of Stamford, which opened in late November 2004.

In July 2006, Beans opened its third shop in Higham Ferrers, Northamptonshire. Venturing into new territory, both in its layout and location, Beans of Higham complimented the other shops perfectly.

2007 saw us open our smallest shop in Oakham, and in June 2008 we moved further south to Olney, 10 miles north of Milton Keynes. Later in 2008 we opened in Uppingham.

However, as with all things in life, all good things come to an end, and we gradually sold off the various shops until in May 2016 we had finally come full circle and just had the original – and clearly the best – cafe in Oundle.

Our Ethics

Fairtrade

Unlike most coffee shop chains ALL of Beans’ coffee is made from freshly ground Fairtrade coffee. We do not use any other kind. Beans works tirelessly to find new and exciting Fairtrade products to stock and replace our existing product range, however we do not compromise on quality. Fairtrade guarantees a fair and stable price for products that enable farmers all over the world to support their families, and crucially, invest in a better future. The Fairtrade mark is an independent label which appears on a wide range of retail and catering products as a guarantee that disadvantaged farmers and workers in the developing world are getting a better deal from the product. You can find out more about Fairtrade at the foundation’s website www.fairtrade.org.uk

Recylcing

Recycling is something we feel very strongly about, we recycle as much plastics, glass and paper as possible and use ethically driven waste management companies. As well as recycling our own waste we also source as many recycled packaging products as we can, and with the introduction of our fully compostable takeaway products – cups, lids, straws and containers – we hope to keep our footprint small and do our bit to make this a sustainable world.

Charity

We are always happy to support local charities, and have an area near the loos dedicated to publicising local charitable or art events. We also love to provide raffle prizes or sponsorship for good causes.

OUR KAREN

So, it’s Beans’ 18th year – yes it’s really been that long! Karen & Phil opened Oundle Beans in February 2004 and since then we’ve gone from strength to strength, and now enjoy (if you will pardon our slight bit of self congratulation) being part of the Oundle fabric! However, 2018 was an extremely sad year for all of us here – customers as well as staff. Karen was the inspiration for the design and decor from the initial opening of our shop up to the present day, for both this and the other Beans cafes. Unfortunately Karen had been battling with cancer since 2002, but she refused to bow to it and carried on as if it was just a common cold. However, after 16 years Karen finally met a match for her stoicism and bravery, with the inevitable outcome. Karen was an inspiration in the truest sense of the word, and we sorely miss her cheerfulness, ideas, organisation and incredible work ethic.

RIP
Karen Lesley Gilbert
1958 – 2018
